Kendall Jenner Wore the Most Unexpectedly Tame Outfit During a Date Night With Bad Bunny

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Wednesday, July 17, 2024

While the KarJenners may not normally be the people to look to when searching for date night dressing inspiration (hi, latex micro-miniskirts and ankle-breaking platform boots!), Kendall Jenner was just spotted on a romantic night out with Bad Bunny in a super chic outfit that was surprisingly tame.

On Wednesday, the pair stepped out at celebrity-loved restaurant Carbone in New York City while wearing coordinating low-key outfits. For Kendall’s part, the supermodel skipped underboob-baring dresses and all-sheer-everything in favor of a silky cream-colored blouse (which she wore buttoned only in the middle) paired with black low-rise pants. Jenner added a simple black leather shoulder bag, tiny gold hoop earrings, and black pointy-toe heels to accessorize her ‘fit, and she rounded out the look by wearing her brunette hair down in loose waves with a middle part.

Bad Bunny kept the neutral vibes going in his outfit, too, by sporting an ivory V-neck blouse paired with black jeans and a New York Yankees baseball cap. The singer styled his ensemble with a pair of black sunglasses and plain black boots, and he added a pop of color by tying a red, black, and white scarf around his face.

The unofficial-but-official couple’s outing comes months after they were first linked to each other back in February. Although it’s clear to fans that the two are definitely together in some capacity (based on numerous public outings), Bad Bunny (whose real name is Benito Antonio Martínez Ocasio) recently opened up about why he refuses to confirm his relationship status publicly when talking to Rolling Stone.

“​​I know something is going to come out," he shared back in June when asked about his relationship with Kendall. "I know [people are] going to say something. People know everything about me, so what's left for me to protect? My private life, my personal life. That's the only answer. In the end, the only thing I have is my privacy.”
